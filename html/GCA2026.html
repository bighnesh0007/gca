About The Conference

We are pleased to announce the "Golden Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Applications (GCAA)", scheduled to take place on 27-29th November 2026 in Dubai in collaboration with the Malaysia University of Science and Technology. GCAA is a peer-reviewed international conference dedicated to bringing together scientists, academics, researchers, and industry professionals to exchange ideas, share high-quality research findings, and present the latest advancements in Artificial Intelligence, Control, Data Sciences, and related applications.

The conference will offer a comprehensive technical program featuring multiple technical sessions showcasing cutting-edge research and technological innovations.

The conference will be held in a hybrid mode, allowing authors to present their papers either physically or online.

All submitted papers will undergo rigorous peer review, and accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion in Springer subject to meeting Springer scope and quality standards.

About Dubai

Dubai has a desert climate, characterized by:

  • Summers (June–September): Extremely hot and humid, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F).
  • Winters (November–March): Pleasant and cooler, ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F), making it the best time for tourism.
  • Rainfall: Minimal, mostly in January and February.

  • Located on the southeast coast of the Persian Gulf, in the United Arab Emirates (UAE).
  • Mostly flat desert terrain, with sandy dunes and coastal areas.
  • Surrounded by the Arabian Desert, with the Hajar Mountains to the east.

Despite its modern transformation, Dubai has a rich history:

  • Bedouin Roots: Originally a small fishing and pearl-diving village.
  • Trade Hub: Flourished due to its strategic location on trade routes.
  • Al Fahidi Historic District: A well-preserved old Dubai area with traditional architecture.
  • Dubai Museum (Al Fahidi Fort): Showcases the city's transformation from a desert outpost to a global metropolis.

Dubai embraces both Emirati traditions and global influences:

  • Burj Khalifa: The world's tallest building.
  • Dubai Opera: A hub for performing arts.
  • Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding: Offers insights into Emirati culture.
  • Jumeirah Mosque: A stunning example of Islamic architecture, open to non-Muslim visitors.
  • Dubai Frame: A massive structure offering views of old and new Dubai.

For adventure seekers and nature lovers:

  • Desert Safari: Dune bashing, camel rides, and Bedouin-style dinners.
  • Skydiving: Over Palm Jumeirah for breathtaking aerial views.
  • Dubai Marina Yacht Tours: Luxurious boat rides along the coastline.
  • Kite Beach: A hotspot for water sports like kite surfing and paddleboarding.
  • Hiking in Hatta: Explore mountains, lakes, and wadis.
  • Deep Dive Dubai: The world's deepest swimming pool for diving enthusiasts.

Publication

Book Publication

All accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion in Springer's Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series, subject to meeting Springer's scope and quality standards.

Papers must be original and not previously published or currently under review elsewhere.

Journal Publication

Selected high-quality papers will be invited for extension and submission to special issues of reputed international journals indexed in SCI, Scopus, and other major databases.

Extended versions must contain at least 30% new material compared to the conference paper.

Submission Guidelines

  • Papers should be written in English and formatted according to the Springer LNCS template.
  • The length of the paper should be between 10-15 pages including figures and references.
  • All papers will undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process.
  • At least one author of each accepted paper must register for the conference and present the paper.

Authors will be notified of acceptance decisions. Accepted papers must be revised according to reviewers' comments and submitted as camera-ready.
