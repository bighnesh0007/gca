About The Conference

We are pleased to announce the "Golden Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Applications (GCAA)", scheduled to take place on 27-29th November 2025 in Malaysia, in collaboration with the Malaysia University of Science and Technology. GCAA is a peer-reviewed international conference dedicated to bringing together scientists, academics, researchers, and industry professionals to exchange ideas, share high-quality research findings, and present the latest advancements in Artificial Intelligence, Control, Data Sciences, and related applications.

The conference will offer a comprehensive technical program featuring multiple technical sessions showcasing cutting-edge research and technological innovations.

The conference will be held in a hybrid mode, allowing authors to present their papers either physically or online.

All submitted papers will undergo rigorous peer review, and accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion in Springer subject to meeting Springer scope and quality standards.

About Malaysia

Malaysia has a tropical rainforest climate, characterized by warm temperatures (25–35°C) and high humidity throughout the year. The country experiences two monsoon seasons:

  • Southwest Monsoon (May – September) – Brings relatively drier conditions.
  • Northeast Monsoon (November – March) – Causes heavy rainfall, particularly on the east coast.

Geographically, Malaysia is divided into Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia (Borneo), separated by the South China Sea. It boasts dense rainforests, stunning beaches, and mountainous regions, with Mount Kinabalu being the highest peak.

Malaysia has a rich history influenced by Malay, Chinese, Indian, and European cultures. Key historical milestones include:

  • The Malacca Sultanate (15th century) – A major trading hub influenced by Islam.
  • Colonial Rule – Ruled by the Portuguese, Dutch, and British before gaining independence in 1957.
  • Formation of Malaysia (1963) – United Malaya, Sabah, Sarawak, and Singapore (which later became independent).

Malaysia is home to diverse cultural landmarks, including:

  • Petronas Twin Towers (Kuala Lumpur) – Iconic skyscrapers with an observation deck.
  • Batu Caves (Selangor) – A Hindu pilgrimage site with a massive golden statue of Lord Murugan.
  • George Town & Malacca (UNESCO Sites) – Colonial-era cities rich in history and heritage.
  • Istana Negara (National Palace) – The official residence of the King of Malaysia.

Malaysia offers exciting outdoor adventures:

  • Hiking & Trekking – Mount Kinabalu, Cameron Highlands, and Taman Negara.
  • Island Hopping & Snorkeling – Langkawi, Perhentian Islands, and Tioman Island.
  • Wildlife Tours – Borneo's rainforests, home to orangutans and proboscis monkeys.
  • Genting Highlands – A hill resort with casinos, theme parks, and cool weather.
  • Legoland Malaysia (Johor Bahru) – A fun family attraction with Lego-themed rides.

Publication

Book Publication

All accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion in Springer's Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series, subject to meeting Springer's scope and quality standards.

Papers must be original and not previously published or currently under review elsewhere.

Journal Publication

Selected high-quality papers will be invited for extension and submission to special issues of reputed international journals indexed in SCI, Scopus, and other major databases.

Extended versions must contain at least 30% new material compared to the conference paper.

Submission Guidelines

  • Papers should be written in English and formatted according to the Springer LNCS template.
  • The length of the paper should be between 10-15 pages including figures and references.
  • All papers will undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process.
  • At least one author of each accepted paper must register for the conference and present the paper.
